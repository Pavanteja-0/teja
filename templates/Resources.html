RESOURCES FOR E-COMMERCE GST SELLERS


◾ The Goods and Services Tax (GST) is an indirect tax—taxation is levied on the supply of goods and services and is ultimately paid by the consumer.
◾ The Government of India introduced the GST in 2017 under their “one nation, one tax” reform. It is a single tax levied on the supply of goods and services right from the manufacturer to the consumer and essentially replaces multiple indirect taxes. The main objective of the reform was to unite different types of taxes in the ambit of a single tax system.

GST return filing for E-commerce Sellers

◾ Sellers who are registering themselves for E-commerce platform should register themselves under GST.
◾ There are several returns to be filed under GST for E-commerce Sellers.
◾ Unlike regular businesses, there is no threshold exemption for the E-commerce Sellers.
◾ GST return form includes the details such as purchases, sales, output GST and Input Tax Credit (ITC) which are to be submitted on monthly, quarterly or annual basis.
◾ Sellers are required to file Form GSTR-1, GSTR 2, GSTR 3 monthly and GSTR-9 is to be filed annually.



GSTR-1

◾ GSTR 1 is a monthly return filed for sales or outward supplies.
◾ The due date for filing GSTR 1 for E-commerce sellers would be 10th day of following month.

GSTR-2

◾ GSTR-2 is a monthly return for inward supplies of goods or services or both received during a tax period.
◾ The due date for GSTR 2 would be 15th of subsequent month.

GSTR-3

◾ GSTR-3 is a consolidated monthly return which contains details of tax liability along with the tax collected on outward supplies and tax paid on inward supplies by registered E-commerce supplier/seller.
◾ The due date for GSTR 3 return filing would be 20th of the next month.

GSTR-9

◾ All the taxpayers registered under GST including E-commerce sellers and operators are required to file their annual return in a particular form.
◾ The due date to file the GSTR-9 is 31st Dec of the preceding year.
